Phyllo Shell

Phyllo is a very thin dough used for making delicious pastries such as baklava, spanakopita, or Albanian pie (aka Byrek). The word phyllo in Greek means "leaf".

Prior to baking you will layer the thin pieces on top of one another. This creates a flaky texture and crunch unlike any other dough. Do not be swayed by those store bought "puff pastry" doughs. Phyllo is the OG and is much tastier, if I do say so myself.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour
  • 1 cup warm water (approx.)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 3/4 pound of melted butter

Steps

  1. Mix flour, salt, and water into a bowl to form the dough
  2. Knead until dough is smooth
  3. Flour dough and cover for 15 mins
  4. Divide dough in half and cut one half into 12 small pieces
  5. Roll into 6-inch circles and butter each one
  6. Stack each on top of the other; do not butter the top circle
  7. Cover with wax paper and refrigerate 15 mins
  8. Repeat the method above with the other half of the dough
  9. Once first half has been refrigerated, roll to fit 15 or 18 inch round pan, which has been greased generously with melted butter
  10. Spread filling evenly. See Byrek Me Spinaq or Byrek Me Arme
  11. Roll out second dough stack, larger than the first and cover the filling
  12. Seal the edges by rolling over or under and sprinkle the top generously with the remaining melted butter
  13. Bake at 400 degrees for approximately 45 mins or until bottom and top crusts are golden brown
